How Did I Get Here?

Thats a though question to answer...

Growing up, my life was marked by constant change. Moving from place to place as a child taught me the invaluable skill of adaptation, not just to new environments but also to the diverse people and cultures I encountered. This ability to embrace change and connect with others has shaped my perspective and approach to life.

Interestingly, I used to despise coding and programming. It seemed like an impenetrable maze of complexities that I was convinced I could never navigate. However, curiosity got the better of me, and one day, I decided to give it a shot on my own. To my surprise, I found an entirely new world waiting for me. Coding became more than just lines of text; it became a canvas for creativity and problem-solving.

My educational journey further fueled my passion. Pursuing the International Baccalaureate (IB) program in high school provided me with a holistic understanding of various subjects, from mathematics and physics to languages and statistics. This diverse knowledge laid a strong foundation for my venture into the realm of programming.

So, how did I get here? Through a blend of adaptability, curiosity, and the willingness to challenge my own preconceptions. The detours and unexpected paths I took ultimately led me to discover my passion for programming, turning a once-dreaded endeavor into an exciting adventure. Now, as an aspiring programmer and enthusiastic learner, I am here, ready to explore the limitless possibilities this journey has to offer.

smplFetch

>>> A simple to use system info fetch script for Linux, which includes basic and commonly used information for the user to display. It also includes - depending on fetch script version - different styles of header art.

ShowMyKeys

>>> ShowMyKeys creates a graphical keyboard using Tkinter and pynput modules, allowing you to visualize your keyboard presses in real-time. The GUI updates to show when a key is pressed or released, giving you a visual representation of your typing, this can be useful for learning to touch type or for streamers wanting to display their speedy combos to their viewers.

Typr: Your Personal Typing Tutor

>>> Typr is a Flutter-based app built in Flet, designed for touch typing instruction, promoting typing best practices, optimizing hand and body posture. It offers various typing tests and tracks your progress in a profile section with an SQL database.
